Aegis — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2, 2026

Architecture

Aegis uses the SimpleX protocol. SimpleX is fundamentally different from Signal, WhatsApp, or Telegram:

  • No user identifiers. No phone number, no username, no account. You are a cryptographic key pair.
  • No contact lists on servers. SimpleX relays don't know who talks to whom. Each conversation uses a different, disposable relay address.
  • No metadata. The relay sees encrypted blobs arriving and leaving. It doesn't know the sender, the recipient, or the conversation.
  • Double ratchet encryption. Same protocol family as Signal, without the phone-number identity layer.

What Aegis stores on your device

  • Your messages (encrypted locally with SQLCipher)
  • Your contacts (local only, never uploaded)
  • Your location (shared only with contacts you explicitly paired with)
  • Security event logs (local only, never transmitted except to your trusted contacts)

What Aegis does NOT collect

  • Phone number
  • Email address
  • Name (unless you type it yourself)
  • Contacts from your phone's address book
  • Browsing history
  • Device identifiers or advertising IDs
  • Analytics, crash data, or telemetry

What SimpleX relays see

Encrypted blobs with disposable addresses. Nothing else. Relays cannot determine the sender, the recipient, or the conversation.

Panic data

During a panic event, Aegis sends GPS coordinates, audio, and camera frames to your trusted contacts only, through the same encrypted SimpleX channels. No server stores this data. No third party sees it.

Duress PIN

The duress PIN opens a decoy profile and silently triggers panic. The existence of the second PIN is cryptographically unprovable.

Data storage

All data is stored locally on your device in an encrypted database (SQLCipher). Nothing is backed up to any cloud. No synchronization service. If you lose your device without a local backup, your data is lost. This is by design.

Data sharing with third parties

None. No data is shared with any third party. No data is sent to the developer. No analytics. No ads. No tracking. No monetization of user data, ever.

Data deletion

Uninstall the app to delete all data. No server-side data exists to delete. Burn-after-reading messages are destroyed after viewing. Disappearing messages are deleted after their timer expires.

Open source

Aegis is open source (AGPL-3.0). The cryptographic protocols (SimpleX, NaCl/libsodium) are well-studied and independently audited. The source code is available at github.com/artst3in/Aegis.
