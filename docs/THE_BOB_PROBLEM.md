# The Bob Problem

*An SOS response simulation in two timelines.*

---

## Setup

**Tuesday, 10:47 PM.**

Bob is at Natasha's apartment. His phone is on the nightstand. His wife Cindy thinks he's at a late work dinner.

Alice — Cindy's best friend, Bob's contact since the wedding — is walking to her car in a parking garage downtown. A man follows her from the elevator.

Alice holds the power button four times.

---

## Timeline A: Bob Accepts

**10:47:03** — Bob's phone screams. Full-screen red. ALICE — SOS. Map shows the parking garage. Live audio: footsteps, Alice breathing fast.

The screen reads: **"You are the closest — 1.2 km. 3 min by car."**

Below: **[I'M RESPONDING]** — *Accepting will share your live location with all contacts in this SOS.*

Bob stares at it. Cindy is in this SOS group. Cindy will see his blue dot at Natasha's address. At 10:47 PM on a Tuesday.

He taps it.

**10:47:08** — Cindy's phone: *"Bob is responding. 1.2 km away. ETA 3 min."* She stares at the map. Bob's dot is on Sadovaya Street. His office is on Pushkin. The late dinner was supposed to be at Milano's on Nevsky. Sadovaya is... residential.

She files that away. Alice comes first.

**10:47:15** — Bob is already in his car, shirtless, one shoe on. He drives like the parking garage is on fire.

**10:49:41** — Bob's car screeches into the garage. The man hears it. Headlights. Engine roar. He runs. Bob finds Alice crouched between two cars, phone in hand, crying.

**10:49:55** — Alice's phone: *"1 responder arrived."*

**10:50:12** — Cindy's phone: *"Bob has arrived at Alice's location."*

Alice is safe.

**11:15 PM** — Bob drives Alice home. Cindy meets them at the door. Hugs Alice. Makes tea. Doesn't say a word about Sadovaya Street.

**Thursday** — Cindy asks for a divorce. She doesn't mention Alice, the SOS, or the rescue. She just says she knows.

Bob saved Alice. Bob lost Cindy. Both were his choice.

---

## Timeline B: Bob Declines

**10:47:03** — Same scream. Same red screen. Same map. Same audio.

**"You are the closest — 1.2 km."**

Bob looks at the button. He looks at Natasha. He puts the phone face-down on the nightstand.

**10:47:30** — Cindy's phone: ALICE — SOS. She's at home, 6.8 km away. She grabs her keys. *"You are the 2nd closest."* No one else has responded.

**10:47:45** — Cindy drives. 6.8 km. Red lights. Traffic. Her hands are shaking.

**10:48:00** — Alice's phone: *"0 responders."*

The man in the garage is closer now.

**10:52:30** — Cindy is still 3 km away. Alice's audio feed goes silent.

**10:56:14** — Cindy arrives. Garage is empty. Alice's phone is on the ground, cracked. Her purse is gone.

**11:30 PM** — Police. Statements. Cindy calls Bob. "Where were you?" He says he was asleep and didn't hear the alert.

Cindy checks the SOS log. Bob received the alert at 10:47:03. He was the closest. 1.2 km. He didn't respond.

She doesn't say anything.

**Friday** — Alice is found. She's alive but hospitalized. Broken wrist, concussion, stolen phone and wallet.

**Saturday** — Cindy checks the SOS log one more time. 1.2 km. 3 minutes. Bob's alert was delivered. Read receipt confirmed. No response.

She asks for a divorce. Not because of Sadovaya Street — she never found out about that. Because Bob let her best friend get beaten in a parking garage while he was "asleep."

Bob kept his secret. Alice paid for it.

---

## The Moral

Both timelines end in divorce.

In Timeline A, Bob is the man who saved Alice and lost his marriage for it. Everyone knows what he did — both things. He walks away broken but clean.

In Timeline B, Bob is the man who let Alice get hurt to protect a lie. Nobody knows about Natasha. Everyone knows he didn't respond. He walks away intact but hollow.

The app didn't judge Bob. It showed him two buttons and let him decide who he is.
