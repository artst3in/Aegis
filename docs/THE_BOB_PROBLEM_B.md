# The Bob Problem — Timeline B

*What the button costs when you don't press it.*

---

## Tuesday, 10:47 PM

Bob is at Natasha's apartment. His wife Cindy thinks he's at a late work dinner.

Alice — Cindy's best friend — is walking to her car in a parking garage downtown. A man follows her from the elevator.

Alice holds the power button four times.

---

**10:47:03** — Bob's phone screams. Full-screen red. ALICE — SOS.

The map shows the parking garage. Live audio: footsteps. Alice breathing fast. A man's voice, low, saying something Bob can't make out.

The screen reads: **"You are the closest — 1.2 km. 3 min by car."**

Below: **[I'M RESPONDING]** — *Accepting will share your live location with all contacts in this SOS.*

Bob stares at the button. Cindy is in this group. Cindy will see his dot. At Natasha's address. At 10:47 on a Tuesday.

He puts the phone face-down on the nightstand. Natasha asks what's wrong. He says nothing, it's just an app notification.

**10:47:30** — Cindy gets the alert. 6.8 km away. She's in pajamas. She grabs her keys and runs.

**10:48:00** — Alice's phone: *"0 responders."*

**10:48:12** — The audio feed picks up Alice screaming. Then the sound of her phone hitting concrete. The audio keeps streaming from the ground.

**10:51:00** — Bob is lying in Natasha's bed. He can still hear it if he picks up his phone. He doesn't pick it up.

**10:56:14** — Cindy arrives at the garage. Third floor. Alice's phone is on the ground, screen cracked, still transmitting. Alice's shoes are six meters away. One of them is broken.

Alice is behind a van. Cindy finds her.

Cindy calls the ambulance. Then she sits on the concrete next to Alice and holds her hand and talks to her and doesn't stop talking until the paramedics arrive.

It doesn't matter. Alice stopped breathing before Cindy got there.

---

## Wednesday

**7:00 AM** — Police confirm it. Alice was sexually assaulted and strangled in the parking garage between 10:48 and 10:53 PM. Five minutes.

Bob was 1.2 km away. Three minutes by car. He knows this because the number is still on his screen.

**9:00 AM** — Cindy calls Bob. She's been at the hospital all night. Her voice is flat. She says Alice is dead. Bob says the right things. His voice shakes in the right places.

**11:00 AM** — Bob goes to the bathroom at work and throws up.

---

## Thursday

The SOS log is shared with police. It shows:

- Alert sent to 8 contacts at 10:47:03
- 0 responders in the first 5 minutes
- Cindy responded at 10:47:34 — 6.8 km away
- Cindy arrived at 10:56:14

It does NOT show Bob's distance. It does not show that he was the closest. It does not show that he read the alert. The app protects his privacy. The app did exactly what it was designed to do.

Bob is protected by the system he refused to use.

---

## Friday — The Funeral

Bob stands next to Cindy. She's destroyed. She keeps saying she should have driven faster. She blames herself for the 9 minutes it took her to arrive.

Bob knows it would have taken him 3.

He holds Cindy's hand and says nothing.

---

## The Weeks After

Bob stops sleeping. Not because anyone suspects him. Nobody does. The log doesn't show his location. Cindy doesn't know. The police don't know. Natasha doesn't know.

Bob knows.

1.2 km. 3 minutes. He was lying in bed and Alice was dying in a parking garage and he chose the bed.

He starts checking the news for the case. The man is caught two weeks later — DNA match from a prior assault. He'd done this before. He'll do life.

That doesn't help. The man being caught doesn't change the 3 minutes. Nothing changes the 3 minutes.

---

## Six Months Later

Bob and Cindy are still married. He never went back to Natasha's. He can't explain why without explaining everything, so he just stopped going.

Cindy starts therapy for the trauma of finding Alice. Bob can't start therapy because he can't tell anyone what he did. What he didn't do. The thing he didn't do is not a crime. It's not even a social failure — nobody knows. It's just a thing that lives in his chest now.

He opens Aegis sometimes and looks at the SOS screen. The button is still there. He's never pressed it.

He is 1.2 km away from Alice for the rest of his life.

---

*The app showed Bob two buttons. Bob chose. Alice is dead. The app didn't kill her. Bob didn't kill her. The man in the garage killed her.*

*But Bob knows what 3 minutes could have changed. And he will know it every morning when he wakes up, and every night when he doesn't sleep, for the rest of his life.*

*That's what the button costs when you don't press it.*
