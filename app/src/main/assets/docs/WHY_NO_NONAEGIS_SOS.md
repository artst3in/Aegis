# Why SOS Needs Aegis

## The short answer

SOS only reaches contacts who also run Aegis. If a contact is on plain
SimpleX, they get nothing — not even a text. That's deliberate, and it
protects *you*, not just the app.

## The request

"My mum doesn't use Aegis, she just has SimpleX. Can't you at least send her
my SOS as a normal message?"

We used to. We removed it. Here's why.

## What a plaintext SOS to a vanilla contact actually is

To deliver an SOS to a non-Aegis client, we'd have to strip it down to an
ordinary chat message — your panic state and your live location, in plain text,
dropped into a normal inbox.

That inbox has none of Aegis's protections:

- **No at-rest sealing.** In Aegis your messages are encrypted behind your PIN
  on the device. A plain SimpleX message isn't — your "I'm in danger, here's
  where I am" sits readable on their phone.
- **No duress handling.** If their phone is taken, unlocked, or shared, your
  alert is right there in the chat.
- **Possibly a monitored device.** The exact situation SOS exists for — a
  controlling partner, a stalker — is one where the *other* phone may be
  watched too. Spraying your location into an unprotected inbox can expose the
  person you're reaching for help.

So "just send her the text" isn't a smaller version of SOS. It's taking the
single most sensitive message Aegis ever sends and publishing it in the clear.

## The cost, stated honestly

If your emergency contact is on plain SimpleX, they will not know you are in
danger. That is real, and it is the point: the way to have a working safety net
is for you **and the people you trust with your life** to run Aegis. Your panic
button is only as strong as the contacts who can actually receive it securely.

## The principle

We don't manufacture a route that leaks your location and your worst moment
into an inbox we can't protect. SOS rides the sealed, Aegis-only channel or it
doesn't ride at all. Tell the people who'd come for you to install the app —
that's the one thing that makes the alert both reach them and stay safe.
