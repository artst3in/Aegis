# Why Aegis Has No Private Messaging in Groups

## The short answer

Aegis has extreme permissions on your phone. Letting a
stranger message you privately through those permissions
is not a risk we will take.

## What Aegis can access

Aegis can access your GPS, camera, microphone, contacts,
and — with Device Owner — can silently install apps, lock
your phone, or factory-reset it. These permissions exist
because they protect you in a crisis.

Every private message from a stranger is a potential
attack surface against all of these permissions.

## The comparison

In a normal messenger, the worst a malicious message can
do is leak your chat history. In Aegis, the worst a
malicious message can do is activate your camera, stream
your microphone, read your GPS, or wipe your phone.

The blast radius is not proportional to the feature.

## What groups are for

Groups in Aegis are community bulletin boards. Anonymous
handles, full conversation, no real names. Discuss safety
tips, share warnings, coordinate with your local community.

You can talk. You cannot be targeted individually.

## How to connect with someone from a group

Exchange contact information outside Aegis — a community
forum, a subreddit, an in-person meetup. Then pair on
Aegis as a 1:1 contact where you choose their trust tier
consciously.

The difficulty of this process is the security feature.
If connecting were easy, stalking would be easy.

## This will not change

No DMs. No whisper. No private messaging in groups.
This is a structural decision, not a missing feature.
